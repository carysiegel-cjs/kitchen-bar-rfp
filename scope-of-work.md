# Handyman Scope of Work — Lower Kitchen Bar / Prep for Countertop

## Project Overview
Modification of existing raised kitchen bar to standard counter height in preparation for new countertop installation.

## Scope of Work

### Preparation
- Protect adjacent floors, cabinets, and work area prior to demolition.

### Demolition
- Remove the existing raised bar cap/overhang and any attached trim or framing above the lower counter height.
- Demolish and dispose of removed materials from the raised bar section.

### Framing
- Cut down and modify the existing stud framing to bring the wall to standard counter height.
- Install a new horizontal top plate across the shortened studs to create a solid, continuous surface for the new countertop.
- Verify the top plate is straight and level across the entire run.

### Electrical
- Relocate existing electrical gang boxes from the raised bar section to the new lower wall face.
- Reinstall boxes using the existing wiring and secure them in the new position.

### Drywall & Finish
- Install new drywall to patch the exposed area created by removal of the raised bar.
- Tape seams and apply joint compound to create a smooth surface.
- Sand patched areas and apply primer as needed.
- Touch-up paint on the modified wall section to match the surrounding finish as closely as practical.

### Final
- Ensure the wall surface and framing are ready for countertop templating and installation.
- Clean the work area and remove all construction debris from the site.

## Notes
- Countertop templating and installation is **not** included in this scope — contractor to prepare surface only.
- Electrical work limited to gang box relocation using existing wiring only. If new wiring is required, contractor must notify owner prior to proceeding.
